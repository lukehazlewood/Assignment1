Displayed below is my Typography task with some short reflection paragraphs about each

Bilateral

Similar to an axial system, a bilateral system, however, centres all of its elements symmetrically on the axis. I focused on creating a fluid body of text that almost build a square shape. This was done by making the text boxes narrow and splitting longer bits of text up.

Axial

In the axial system, all elements must be aligned to the left or right side of the central axis, I wanted to make mine more interesting by aligning my contents diagonally and adding some shapes as well.

Transitional

For transitional the elements are layered in an informal manner, I decided to put my text within wavy lines through adobe illustrator

Radial

Within this system the text must extend from a central point of focus, I decided to make my text extend from an invisible circle, however I think next time I could focus more on the positioning of my text to really make it wrap around said “circle”

Random

Random really has no rules, in this I wanted to have my text displayed in a chaotic manner whilst also ensuring that the title was still legible, it takes a longer time to read the text layered underneath however, so next time I would like to find techniques that would make this more possible

modular

Modular layouts use repeating structures to break up the content, for this I opted toward putting my text inside circles whilst also having the main information positioned off centre at the top of the box.

Grid

Grid is relatively simple to explain, all elements are aligned onto grids, for this I made two small square bodies of text and separated them by having two other grid elements, squares.

Dilatational

Elements are displayed in a circular structure within the dilatational system, I had the circle displaying the name of the Arnolfini centre positioned on the outermost circle due to the importance, I also had it displayed in bold to make it more noticeable.
